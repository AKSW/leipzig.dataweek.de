09:15

Communication is key: Why Data Scientists Must Be Able to Do More Than Code & Data

09:15 - 09:45 | Festsaal | german

Kevin Trebing (Plan D) and Raana Saheb Nassagh (Plan D)

In our talk, we describe the small and big pitfalls that can occur in the life of a Data Scientist. Coming from the “safe space” of the university, where you can tinker around in your own Git repository, you suddenly have to work with many other people in your everyday professional life. “Hell, it’s the others,” says Sartre. Well, it may not be quite that bad. Still, it makes sense to deal with the different perspectives and expectations of everyone involved. How do I communicate with platform architects and data protection officers? What do I need to consider in customer contact? What do my superiors expect from me - and I from them? Am I allowed to make mistakes? Which tools and procedures help in collaboration? Using concrete examples, we show why Data Scientists need to be able to do much more than analyze data and write code. And why exactly that makes the job so exciting.

17:00

Workshop Semantics: Developer perspective

17:00 - 18:20 | Ratsplenarsaal | english

Alejandro Villar and Rob Atkinson

Hands-on session with some of the tools used and/or developed by OGC.

We will demonstrate how to create CI/CT/CD semantic uplift pipelines to convert different types of input documents (CSV, JSON) into RDF (JSON-LD, Turtle), including the use of SHACL for semantic entailment (data enrichment and relationship inference) and validation, from scratch.

We will also give a tour of our developer tools and libraries available to the community, such as the OGC Playground, the ready-to-use GitHub repository templates and the OGC Rainbow Docker containers.

Finally, the topic of specification generation pipelines will be covered.
